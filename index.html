Professional Summary

I'm a genomics and bioinformatics researcher with expertise in human genetics, gene expression analysis, and data analysis. My aim is to integrate large-scale omics data with biological interpretation, investigating how genes interact, regulate one another, and drive complex heritable diseases and cancer. My work has employed genetic markers, such as cell-free DNA and cell-free RNA, in the assessment of various pathological conditions.

I am also interested in the genomic exploration of non-model organisms to understand their diversity, genome structure, and the functional impacts of genetic variation. In this area, I have worked with high-throughput sequencing datasets to characterize genetic diversity and metagenomic landscapes, extract biological insights, and support the selection of genetic markers — particularly for aquaculture applications.

Human & medical genomics

Transcriptome & miRNome analysis, cell-free DNA/RNA as biomarkers, transplant monitoring, and variant discovery for heritable disease.

Aquatic & comparative genomics

De novo genome assembly, developmental transcriptomics, metagenomics, and genomic selection for aquaculture improvement.
